This venue served as an oasis for Black entertainers and patrons and openly defied the segregation laws of the 1940s to 1960s. Irma Thomas, Deacon John, Clarence “Frogman” Henry, and Allen Toussaint talk about the Dew Drop Inn during the era of segregation and the Dew Drop’s role in the fight for Civil Rights.

Music: “Let’s Sing”, James Booker
